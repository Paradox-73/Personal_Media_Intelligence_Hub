## Diagnosis: three bugs in `get_umap_data`

**Bug 1 — Unscaled, mixed-magnitude features under a euclidean metric.** The code takes *all* 402 columns raw: `year` (~1950–2026), runtime in minutes (~30–200), log vote counts, 0/1 multi-hots, and PCA vibe components (~±2). With euclidean distance, year and runtime dominate the metric by an order of magnitude. The string/filament structures are the signature: UMAP's neighbor graph degenerates into chains where each point's nearest neighbors are simply the items adjacent in *year*, so the manifold collapses to year-sorted threads. Hover along one of those arcs and I'd bet the release years increase monotonically. That's not taste topology, it's a timeline rendered as spaghetti.

**Bug 2 — The space contains the features that *define* domain membership.** Your own expander says the vector includes "Media Type markers," and the unified schema has `has_{domain}_feats` masks plus large domain-specific blocks that are `nan_to_num`'d to zero for every other domain. Music rows differ from movie rows in *hundreds* of coordinates purely by missingness pattern. Domain separation is therefore guaranteed by construction — the music blob would appear even if your music and movie tastes were identical. This is the same domain-stratification problem we diagnosed in the earlier UMAPs, except now it's baked in twice: once by masks, once by explicit media-type one-hots. Note also CORAL alignment isn't applied anywhere in this script, so the page is visualizing the *unaligned* space you've already shown to be confounded.

**Bug 3 — The title join is positional and almost certainly misaligned.** `pd.concat([df, titles_df[['title']].reset_index(drop=True)], axis=1)` assumes the enriched CSVs, loaded independently, have exactly the same row count and order as the unified training matrix. Any filtering during feature engineering (dropped NaN rows, the 'Incomplete' games handling, dedup) shifts every label after that point — so hover titles can be silently wrong, which is worse than missing. Joins must be on `source_id`, never position. Quick falsification test: hover a few points you know well and check the year in the tooltip matches the title.

Smaller issues: the slider filters music by `target_reg`, which for music is a PU pseudo-label, not a rating — labeling it "Predicted/Actual Rating" is misleading; and with 3,688 of 4,952 points being music, the map is 74% pseudo-labeled items visually drowning the rated library.
